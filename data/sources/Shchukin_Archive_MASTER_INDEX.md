# THE SHCHUKIN COLLECTION ARCHIVE
## Master Index & System of Findings
*Consolidated 13 June 2026 · Kunst Gruppe Bureau / Shchukin Foundation*

This is the top-level index to the entire archive. It systematises every finding into one structure: **a dynasty of collectors across six generations of activity (1818–present), four historic collections, one modern collection, and the institutions that carry the name today** — each documented to the depth the sources allow, and each tagged by evidence tier so the archive stays defensible in scholarly or legal use.

---

## 1 · Architecture

The archive is organised in **layers**, mirroring the actual history rather than a flat list:

| Layer | Scope | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **0 · Origins** | Ivan Vasilyevich Shchukin (1818–1890) & the Botkin marriage — the textile fortune that funded everything | Context |
| **A · Historic brothers** | Pyotr, **Sergei**, Dmitri, Ivan — the canonical Shchukin collections, now in Russian state museums | Sergei object-complete; others guide-level |
| **B · Modern chapter** | Nikolai Shchukin (1953–2024) & Gallery SHCHUKIN — the 20th–21st-c. revival | Chapter-level |
| **C · Contemporary** | Dmitry Shchukin, the present brand, fairs and forward activity | Institutional |

The **master entity register** (`shchukin_entities.csv` / `.json`) holds all ten entities in machine-readable form with role, dates, scale, location, evidence tier, and catalogue status.

## 2 · Method — the evidence-tier system

One discipline runs through every chapter. Each claim is classed as:

- **[Public]** — independently documented in scholarship, museum records, press, or institutional publication.
- **[Family-asserted]** — stated publicly by the family or gallery and repeated in press, but **not** independently corroborated (e.g. the modern family's descent from the historic Shchukins).
- **[Estate]** — held in family/estate records, true to first-hand knowledge but not on the open web (e.g. Nikolai's dates, the NACFUND founding year).

This is the same provenance logic applied to objects: state what is documented, flag what is asserted, and never let the second masquerade as the first. It is what makes the archive usable if a scholar, buyer, or court ever tests it.

## 3 · Master timeline

- **1818** — Ivan Vasilyevich Shchukin born; builds I.V. Shchukin & Sons (textiles).
- **1853 / 1854 / 1855 / 1869** — Pyotr / Sergei / Dmitri / Ivan born.
- **1893** — Ivan settles in Paris; becomes the family's conduit to French art.
- **1898** — Sergei & Pyotr buy Pissarros side by side at Durand-Ruel.
- **1903** — Dmitri donates 19 Old Master pictures to the Moscow University museum.
- **1905** — Pyotr donates his Museum of Russian Antiquities to the Historical Museum.
- **1908** — Ivan's bankruptcy and suicide; his collection dispersed at Paris sales.
- **1913** — Sergei publishes his collection catalogue (225 numbers).
- **5 Nov 1918** — Sergei's gallery nationalised → First Museum of New Western Painting.
- **1923 / 1948** — GMNZI formed / dissolved; Sergei's works split Hermitage ↔ Pushkin.
- **1936 / 1932 / 1912** — deaths of Sergei / Dmitri / Pyotr.
- **1953** — Nikolai Vasilyevich Shchukin born. *[Estate]*
- **1987** — Gallery SHCHUKIN founded; Nikolai revives the collecting tradition. *[Public]*
- **1997** — National Art Collections Foundation founded. *[Estate]*
- **2002–2004** — Art Moscow album; Moscow avant-garde exhibitions (Dmitry).
- **2013** — Gallery SHCHUKIN Paris opens on Avenue Matignon.
- **2014–2015** — "In Other Worlds" (Drutt) in New York; Gleb Pospelov Hall.
- **2019** — Pushkin's "Shchukin. Biography of a Collection" reunites all four brothers.
- **2024** — Nikolai Shchukin dies. *[Estate]*
- **2025–2026** — present custodianship and archive (this project).

## 5 · Provenance spine (Sergei — the model)

Every Sergei object carries one chain:
**S. Shchukin → nationalisation 5 Nov 1918 → First Museum of New Western Painting → GMNZI (1923) → 1948 dissolution → Hermitage *or* Pushkin.**
Flagships are gold-verified with **dual numbering** — current accession + historical ГМНЗИ number (e.g. *Dance* ГЭ-9673 / ГМНЗИ-98; *Music* ГЭ-9674 / ГМНЗИ-97; *Three Women* ГЭ-9658 / ГМНЗИ-254). The ГМНЗИ number is the provenance fingerprint linking any object to the 1923–48 museum.

## 6 · Image sourcing — master reference

Applies to every collection. Best single technique: **Wikimedia Commons `Special:FilePath/{filename}`** returns the original full-resolution file.

- **Commons categories:** `Sergey Schukin's collection`; `Paintings in the Pushkin Museum`; `Google Art Project works in the Pushkin`.
- **NeWestMuseum viewer:** `newestmuseum.ru/full/index.php?id={GE_xxxx}&coll=1` (Sergei works with known inv. id).
- **Museum portals:** hermitagemuseum.org/digital-collection; collections.hermitage.ru; pushkinmuseum.art; catalog.shm.ru (Pyotr); goskatalog.ru (all).
- **Google Arts & Culture** — Pushkin & Hermitage partner pages (gigapixel zoom).
- The Sergei CSV embeds a per-work direct hi-res link (23 works) and a Commons lookup link (all 259).
